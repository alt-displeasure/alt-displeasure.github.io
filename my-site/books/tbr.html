For the past 2 or 3 years, as I've tried reconnecting with my reading hobby, I discovered I really like historical fiction (is that what this genre is called? What I mean is realistic fictional books set at least a few decades ago). I've read and loved most of Jane Auten's books, and I really liked politically heavy books like Harper Lee's To Kill a Mockingbird and Margaret Atwood's The Handmaid's Tale. Overall, I've been enjoying reading many classics, so if you have any similar suggestions feel free to tell me (to be considered: I have already read many classics, like some of Orwell's books, a little Shakespeare, Fahrenheit 451, The Great Gatsby and a few others that aren't listed on my "Finished" list, as I've prioritized my most recent reads.)

Surprisingly for me, I've found that I also quite enjoy non-fiction historical and/or political books. From The Prince to Argentinian feminist Florencia Freijo's books, I've had a great time reading these essay-books, although I have to confess they are a bit of a heavy read to pair with my studies and I can only read them if I'm well-rested (rare).

In a completely unrelated book genre, I've also given Terry Pratchett's Discworld books a try and I've loved them so far (I've only read 2 and I'm trying to get a physical copy of the third). I think my liking of his books might be the only legacy left over from my fantasy-filled childhood. Apart from that, as I've grown older I've found it harder and harder to like excessively fansastic and magical books...
